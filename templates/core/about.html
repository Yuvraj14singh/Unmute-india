WHY WE EXIST

Too many students stay silent because speaking can feel unsafe, public or difficult.

A student should not need a perfect explanation, a public identity or a brave face before someone is willing to listen.

Unmute India lowers that first barrier. A student can begin privately in text, audio, image or video; learn from moderated public voices; seek a practical next step; or support a documented call for change.

HOW LISTENING WORKS

Human care with clear boundaries.

1

You share in your own way

Write, record audio, add an image or use video. A face and real name are never required.

2

Your message enters a private workflow

It does not become a public post. Authorised members access it through controlled tools.

3

A thoughtful response can follow

Peer listening is offered without diagnosis, forced advice or pressure to keep talking.

PRIVATE BY DEFAULT

Support is not permission to publish.

Private emotional submissions and public Unmuted Voices are deliberately separate. Public sharing requires an explicit choice, a privacy check and staff approval.

WHY ACCOUNTABILITY BELONGS HERE

Listening to students also means recording what institutions must answer.

Care supports the individual. Accountability examines the system.

The platform keeps petitions, evidence, timelines, public questions and authority responses source-led and reviewable. Verified support is counted carefully; unverified claims are not presented as established fact.

WHAT GUIDES US

Trust should come from how the platform behaves.

Listen before advising

Meet the student where they are and ask what kind of response would help.

Protect before publishing

Keep private content out of public spaces unless the required consent and review are complete.

Verify before claiming

Use sources, dates and transparent status instead of viral certainty.

Be honest about limits

Peer support is not emergency, medical, legal or clinical care.

CLEAR LIMITS

What Unmute India is—and is not.

We are

  • A private-by-default student listening space
  • A moderated library of consented public voices
  • A practical support starting point
  • An evidence-led accountability platform
  • A collective student and volunteer mission

We are not

  • An emergency or crisis-response service
  • A replacement for licensed professional care
  • A public feed for private submissions
  • A place where allegations become facts without evidence
  • A personality-led or founder-focused brand
