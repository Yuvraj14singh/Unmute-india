WHY ACCOUNTABILITY MATTERS

When an exam system fails, the impact does not remain on paper.

It enters homes, classrooms and the lives of students who have spent years preparing.

01

Exam failures

Administrative breakdowns can disrupt preparation, admissions and trust.

02

Leak allegations

Every allegation deserves a timely, independent and evidence-based examination.

03

Re-exam stress

Uncertainty can force students and families to carry another cycle of financial and emotional pressure.

04

Communication gaps

Delayed or unclear information creates rumours where transparent updates should exist.

05

Student wellbeing

Results, delays and uncertainty have a human impact that institutions must not dismiss.

06

Top-level responsibility

Students deserve to know who is responsible for correcting failures and preventing repetition.

These are areas of public concern—not declarations of legal guilt. Verified claims and primary sources belong in the timeline and evidence archive below.

PEACEFUL PROTEST & STUDENT RIGHTS

Know the boundary between guidance and legal advice.

Students should understand peaceful protest, evidence preservation, reporting misconduct and what to do if detained. Guidance must be reviewed, jurisdiction-aware and clear about its limits.

  • Stay peaceful and follow lawful directions.
  • Preserve original files, dates and context.
  • Tell a trusted person where you are.
  • Seek a verified legal professional for personal legal advice.

General information is not a substitute for advice from a qualified lawyer.
